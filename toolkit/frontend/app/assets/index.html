Modelling and Network Analysis of Intronic miRNA mediated Gene Expression Regulation

The regulation of gene expression by microRNA has been known for some time. It has also been seen that intronic miRNA govern certain processes in the cell that can generate a feedback mechanism for the amount of proteins that will be formed depending upon the protein requirement by the cell.

This tool facilitates the network generation, visualisation, and simulation.

Enter a few Genes or miRNAs (or either) in the input on the left. The evolving regulation network will be grown in real time.

Example Gene Input: 'CDKN1A', 'TOB2', 'Keratin', etc.
Example miRNA Input: 'hsa-miR-148b-5p', 'hsa-mir-184', 'hsa-miR-1254', etc.
